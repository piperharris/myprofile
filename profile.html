Personal Profile

I undertook the Myers Briggs Type Indicator Test, the Learning Styles Test and the Leadership test in order to evaluate my potential performance in team situations.

Myers Briggs Type Indicator Test

The Protagonist (EFNJ-T)

Outcome: ENFJ-T (The Protagonist)

I was surprised by the results of my Myers Briggs Type Indicator Test (MBTI) as I would personally classify myself as an introvert. However, after reading into the difference between introverts and extraverts, I have realised that it is not solely based on how an individual acts around people, but also the environments and situations they find themselves in.

Percentages of each category of the Myers Briggs Test.

Learning Styles Test

Outcome: Mostly Pragmatist Style

To be completely honest, I did not even know what pragmatism was until I took this test, however now knowing the definition I can confirm that I do take a practical approach to my learning. I like to learn through trial and error, using problem solving to better my understanding of a subject.

Ledership Test

Outcome: Transformative Leader

After learning about the characteristics of a transformative leader, I feel like this style fits me well, as I would want to be a part of a team with this structure. Transformative leaders encourage members to create change and constantly improve themselves by giving them space for innovation to create solutions for future problems.

Evaluation

Both the MBTI and the Leadership tests have indicated to me that I am very much a team player and would much rather work in a collaborative manner than individually. I agree with these results to a significant extent as I like to have people who I can share my ideas with and hear their own ideas and opinions, creating a positive attitude towards team related work. I think it is important, especially in a rapidly growing field such as IT, to have other people you can bounce thoughts and ideas off to create the ultimate outcome and solutions you can.
